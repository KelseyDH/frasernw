Routine Pregnancy Care Schedule

Obstetrics Gynecology

Week Intervention Notes
0–14 Folic acid 0.4–5 mg daily Risk dependent
Routine blood tests: blood group, Rh and RBC antibody, Hb, MCV, HIV, rubella titre, STS, HbsAg, TSH, chlamydia and gonorrhea test, urine C&S
Additional blood tests if indicated: HCV, GTT/FBG, Pap. If exposure – Parvo B19, mumps, toxoplasmosis, CMV
Counsel all women regarding alcohol and tobacco use Use TWEAK questionnaire for alcohol
Administer inactivated vaccines if needed: flu, Hep, DPT Avoid live/attenuated vaccines: MMR, Varicella
Complete Antenatal Record/Physical. Each visit: BP, U/A for proteinuria, weight If appropriate: STI screen, SFH, FHR
For questions about drugs in pregnancy see Motherrisk Email links to patient
Give patient information about a healthy pregnancy from The Best Chance Also see Baby's Best Chance video set. Email links to patient
10–13
    Offer genetic screening (SIPS). Results inform patient of chance of baby having:
  • Down syndrome
  • trisomy 18
  • open nerual tube defect
    SIPS part 1: 10-13 weeks, SIPS part 2: 15-20 weeks.
  • Requisition
  • Patient information
  • Other Languages
  • Visual aid
11–14 Offer early U/S to confirm dates
Offer additional genetic screening (NT) for women at higher risk of Down pregnancy

Nuchal translucency (NT) ultrasound to be done in conjunction with first and second trimester serum markers (IPS)

    Eligibility:
  • women > 35  at (EDD)
  • twin pregnancies
  • HIV positive
  • history of a previous child or fetus with Down syndrome, trisomy 18 or trisomy 13
  • (IVF) withintracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI)
    Resources:
  • Nuchal Translucency sites
  • Summary of timing of all prenatal genetic screenings
14–20 Offer genetic screening (QUAD) to patients who present for first visit after 13 weeks See prenatal screening table
18–20 Offer detailed ultrasound
24–26 Repeat Rh antibody if Rh negative
24–28 Consider GDM screen if risk factors
28 Offer Rh Ig to Rh negative women Blood product – consent
28–32 Administer EPDS depression screening
34 Assess fetal presentation If unsure – get U/S. Consider ECV
35–37 Offer GBS testing If pt Pen anaphylaxis, request sensitivities
36–40 Genital HSV suppression If recurrent: Acyclovir 400 tid from week 36 until delivery
37–41 Review/Discuss GBS results & options
> 41 Induction consideration
