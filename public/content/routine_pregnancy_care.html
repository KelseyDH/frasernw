Routine Pregnancy Care Schedule

Obstetrics Gynecology

Week Intervention Notes
0–14 Offer genetic screening (SIPS) 1st blood test must be done before 14 weeks. Test for PAPP-A
Folic acid 0.4–5 mg daily Risk dependent
Offer early U/S to confirm dates Between 11–14 weeks
Counsel all women regarding alcohol and tobacco use TWEAK questionnaire for alcohol
Inactivated vaccines: flu, Hep, DPT if needed Avoid live/attenuated vaccines: MMR, Varicella
Complete Antenatal Record/Physical
Test: blood group, Rh and RBC antibody, Hb, MCV, HIV, rubella titre, STS, HbsAg, TSH, chlamydia and gonorrhea test, urine C&S If indicated: HCV, GTT/FBG, Pap. If exposure – Parvo B19, mumps, toxoplasmosis, CMV
Each visit: BP, U/A for proteinuria, weight If appropriate: STI screen, SFH, FHR
15–17 Genetic screening (Quad screen) 2nd blood test between 15 and 20 weeks; test for AFP, Estradiol, hCG, and inhibin A
18–20 Offer detailed ultrasound
24–26 Repeat Rh antibody if Rh negative
24–28 Consider GDM screen if risk factors
28 Offer Rh Ig to Rh negative women Blood product – consent
28–32 Administer EPDS Depression screen
34 Assess fetal presentation If unsure – get U/S. Consider ECV
35–37 Offer GBS testing If pt Pen anaphylaxis, request sensitivities
36–40 Genital HSV suppression If recurrent: Acyclovir 400 tid from week 36 until delivery
37–41 Review/Discuss GBS results & options
> 41 Induction consideration
